Non-Locals

5/7 say it’s important to have foreign talent to fill up the gaps in Singaporean workforce.

4/7 non-locals view Singaporeans as rigid, disciplined, and focused citizens.

3/7 non-locals think that Singaporeans are too busy to be friendly, while 4/7 of them think that Singaporeans are indeed friendly.

3/7 non-locals don’t feel discrimination in Singapore, whereas 4/7 of them believe that discrimination is only against those who are not Caucasians.

After conducting our research, we decided to target the non-locals in Singapore in order to foster a caring, gracious and inclusive society because:

  • Singaporeans are too shy to make the first move. However when approached, they would willingly help or even chat if they have the time.
  • Singaporeans do not hate non-locals. In fact, they believe that Singapore needs foreign talent for to prosper.
  • Non-locals view Singaporeans as rigid and disciplined. This mindset can be changed if they learnt more about the Singaporean culture and understood the locals.
  • Non-locals may have false perceptions of Singaporeans, which may hinder them from trying to befriend the locals.

We also believe that it would be easier for non-locals to understand our culture, rather than the reverse, due to the years of heritage and behaviour we have embedded into our society, and also due to our rigidity.

I Wish connects locals and non-locals with similar interests.
